#### D. Tabletop Exercise Template
**File**: `templates/TABLETOP_EXERCISE_TEMPLATE.md`

**Structure**:
1. Learning Objectives (4 objectives covering knowledge, application, analysis, perspective)
2. Scenario Setup (2-3 pages: date, situation, timeline, context, uncertainty)
3. Factions (4 factions typically):
   - Faction 1: United States Government
   - Faction 2: China
   - Faction 3: Allied Countries (EU, Japan, South Korea)
   - Faction 4: [Relevant additional actor]
4. Three-Move Structure:
   - Move 1: Initial Response (15 min)
   - Move 2: Escalation/Complications (20 min)
   - Move 3: Resolution/Critical Decision (20 min)
5. Debrief Questions (20-30 min): Effectiveness, constraints, information, consequences, escalation, real-world parallels, policy lessons
6. Facilitator Guide: Preparation, roles, pitfalls, adaptations, variations

**Worked Example**: "Critical Minerals Coercion - The Rare Earth Crisis of 2026" (full scenario with Chinese export controls on rare earths in retaliation for US semiconductor restrictions)

**Time**: 60-90 minutes per exercise

**Target**: 1 comprehensive exercise per chapter (10 total)

## Key Innovations and Differentiators

### 1. **Multi-Perspectival Approach**
Unlike most US-centric national security texts, this book systematically includes:
- **Chinese perspectives** grounded in their strategic culture, history, and official documents
- Use of Chinese-language sources and concepts
- Recognition of legitimate Chinese interests and grievances (without endorsing all positions)
- **Strategic empathy**: Understanding adversary logic to predict behavior and design effective strategy

**Value**: Produces strategists who can "think like Beijing" to anticipate responses and avoid surprise

---

### 2. **Practitioner Focus**
Every chapter includes actionable reference material:
- **Government Tools Boxes**: Detailed legal authorities with recent examples
- **Data Sources**: How to access real-time tracking and databases
- **Tabletop Exercises**: Hands-on decision-making practice

**Value**: Book serves as both teaching text AND desk reference for actual policymakers

---

### 3. **Integration of Tools and Strategy**
Unlike books that focus only on tools (sanctions, export controls) OR only on strategy (grand strategy, IR theory), this integrates:
- How specific tools work legally and economically
- When they are/aren't effective
- How they fit into broader strategic competition
- Trade-offs between competing objectives

**Value**: Bridges gap between technical experts and strategic thinkers

---

### 4. **Systematic Case Study Approach**
Every case study includes:
- Background and objectives
- Tools employed
- Target response
- Outcomes and effectiveness assessment
- **Lessons learned** (generalizable insights)
- Multiple perspectives on same event

**Value**: Move beyond description to extract usable lessons

---

### 5. **Interactive Learning**
Tabletop exercises provide:
- Active learning through scenario-based decision-making
- Multi-faction gameplay forcing perspective-taking
- Fog of war (incomplete information, time pressure)
- Cascading consequences showing second/third-order effects
- Structured debrief connecting experience to concepts

**Value**: Better retention and deeper understanding than passive reading

## Quality Standards Established

### Writing Guidelines
- **Voice**: Professional but accessible; active voice preferred
- **Objectivity**: "The United States" rather than "we" for analytical distance
- **Citations**: All factual claims cited; consistent format
- **Accessibility**: Jargon explained; acronyms defined; clear structure

### Content Standards
- **Perspective boxes**: Substantive (800-1500 words), not token additions
- **Case studies**: Detailed enough for real lessons, not just summaries
- **Tools boxes**: Accurate legal citations, recent examples, honest effectiveness assessment
- **Exercises**: Realistic scenarios, plausible options, meaningful trade-offs

### Version Control Standards
- **Commit frequency**: End of each writing session
- **Commit messages**: Clear and descriptive
- **Branching**: Chapter branches for active work; merge to develop when complete
- **Documentation**: PROJECT_PLAN.md tracks progress

### Potential Risks Identified:

**Risk 1: Scope Creep**
- **Mitigation**: Stick to chapter outlines; resist urge to add sections mid-writing; defer "nice to have" content to second edition

**Risk 2: Chinese Perspective Quality**
- **Challenge**: Ensuring depth and accuracy without being native Mandarin speaker
- **Mitigation**:
  - Rely on Chinese-to-English translations by experts
  - Consult scholars like Jude Blanchette, Matt Sheehan, etc.
  - Use authoritative translations of official documents
  - Have Chinese perspective boxes reviewed by China experts

**Risk 3: Time Slippage**
- **Challenge**: Chapters may take longer than 2 weeks each
- **Mitigation**:
  - Front-load work on critical chapters (1-8)
  - Chapters 9-10 could be simplified if needed
  - Weekly progress tracking in PROJECT_PLAN.md
  - Build buffer time into 28-week timeline

**Risk 4: Data Access**
- **Challenge**: Some proprietary databases may be inaccessible
- **Mitigation**:
  - Identify free/open alternatives early
  - Use publicly available think tank data compilations
  - Document limitations where necessary
