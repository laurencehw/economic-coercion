## Corrections Applied

### 1. Chapter 6: Trade Controls and Tariffs

**Line 119 - U.S. Import Data Correction**
- **Previous:** "U.S. imports from China fell from $539 billion (2018) to $427 billion (2019), a 21% decline"
- **Corrected:** "U.S. imports from China fell from $539.5 billion (2018) to $452.2 billion (2019), a 16.2% decline (U.S. Census Bureau 2020)"
- **Source:** U.S. Census Bureau trade data

### 2. Chapter 7: Financial Statecraft and Sanctions

**Line 9 - SWIFT Payment Percentage (Data Consistency Fix)**
- **Previous:** "invoicing currency (40% of SWIFT payments)"
- **Corrected:** "invoicing currency (42% of SWIFT payments)"
- **Rationale:** Aligned with other references in the chapter (Lines 65, 98) and Chapter 10

### 3. Chapter 9: Historical and Comparative Cases

**Line 105 - Soviet Grain Embargo Statistics**
- **Previous:** "Total 1980 imports reached 34.8 million tons (vs. 31 million in 1979 before embargo)"
- **Corrected:** "Despite the embargo, Soviet grain imports in the 1979/80 marketing year reached approximately 31 million metric tonsâ€”only 5-6 million tons short of their pre-embargo target of 36-37 million tons (Paarlberg 1980; USDA 1981)"
- **Source:** USDA historical data, Heritage Foundation analysis

**Line 198 - South African Apartheid Demographics**
- **Previous:** "Whites (13% of population) controlled 90% of economy"
- **Corrected:** "Whites (approximately 20% of population) owned 87% of the land and dominated 90% of the formal economy, while the Black majority was confined to just 13% of the country's territory"
- **Rationale:** The 13% figure conflated white population percentage with land allocated to Black homelands

### 4. Chapter 10: Future Scenarios and Policy Implications

**Line 11 - U.S.-China Bilateral Trade**
- **Previous:** "China-U.S. bilateral trade remains $690 billion annually"
- **Corrected:** "China-U.S. bilateral goods trade remains approximately $575-580 billion annually, down from the 2022 peak of $690 billion"
- **Source:** USTR 2024 data

**Line 43 - Reserve Currency Percentage**
- **Previous:** "Dollar comprises ~60% of global foreign exchange reserves"
- **Corrected:** "Dollar comprises ~58% of global foreign exchange reserves (down from 71% in 2000) (IMF COFER 2024)"
- **Source:** IMF COFER database Q4 2024

**Line 68 - CIPS Transaction Volume**
- **Previous:** "processing $13+ trillion annually (2024), growing 50%+ yearly"
- **Corrected:** "processing approximately $24 trillion annually (RMB 175 trillion in 2024), with 40-43% year-on-year growth, though still relying on SWIFT messaging for over 80% of transactions (PBOC 2025)"
- **Source:** PBOC official data, FXC Intelligence analysis

### 5. Chapter 2: Supply Chain Vulnerabilities

**Line 50 - Rare Earth Processing (Data Consistency Fix)**
- **Previous:** "processes roughly 85% of rare earths"
- **Corrected:** "processes roughly 85-90% of rare earths into usable forms (USGS 2023)"
- **Rationale:** Aligned with Line 214 reference and authoritative source

## Remaining Recommendations

### High Priority Items for Future Review

1. **Antibiotic Dependency Figures (Ch 2)**: The "90-95% of antibiotics from China" claim warrants further verification. Recent academic studies (PMC 2024) suggest approximately 60-70% of antibiotic APIs are imported from China.

2. **TSMC Arizona Cost Premium (Ch 2, 4)**: The "30-50% higher costs" claim shows variation in sources. Morris Chang cited 50%; TechInsights analysis suggests 10-20%. Consider adding nuance.

3. **Big Fund Citation (Ch 4)**: The "Triolo and Greene 2023" citation for Big Fund III could not be verified. Big Fund III was announced in May 2024. Recommend updating citation to 2024 sources.

4. **Chinese VC Investment Data (Ch 5)**: The $9.9B to $1B decline figures conflate different metrics. Recommend clarifying methodology.

5. **Aircraft Export Decline (Ch 6)**: The "47%" figure needs specific sourcing. Available data shows ~43% dollar value decline or 72% unit count decline.

6. **Energy Export Decline (Ch 6)**: The "54%" figure could not be independently verified. Consider revising or adding source.

### Medium Priority

1. Add IMF COFER database as standard citation for reserve currency percentages
2. Add BIS Triennial Survey citation for FX transaction data
3. Update all "as of 2024" data points with specific quarter references
4. Create data dictionary for `/data/sources/` CSV files documenting provenance

- Major news organizations for verification of cited claims
